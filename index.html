Why Play Zilean as a Support?

Do you enjoy:

  • Throwing clocks and bombs at people
  • Playing a champion that tests the intelligence of your enemies
  • Outplaying your opponents through clever usage of skills
  • Playing a champion that can carry from damage OR utility
  • Having an incredible zone of control
  • Being a stalwart enabler for teamwork
  • The ability to reverse your teammate's screw ups
  • Zipping around the map at over 600 movement speed
  • Being able to prolong, possibly even prevent death
  • Winning games that you should have lost

If you enjoy any or all of these things, I invite you to learn more so you can become the Zilgod and gain the admiration of your team as you carry them to victory, all as a support.

The road to becoming a Zilgod is not easy, and requires time and dedication.

Pros and Cons of Support Zilean

Pros

  • Zones well and deals admirable poke damage
  • Can turn a teamfight by saving a teammate with Ultimate
  • Extremely low cooldown on Ultimate with CDR
  • Scales well with levels, extremely strong ultity as game goes on
  • Excellent gank support through crowd control
  • Versatile build orders and skill orders
  • Low cost for optimal utility build

Cons

  • High mana costs for essential combos
  • Vulnerable to early all-ins pre level 6
  • Landing spell combos takes time to master
  • Difficult to play at higher ping (100ms+)
  • Dependent on semi-competent team
  • Requires a bit of in-game knowledge to time Ultimate perfectly
  • If enemies are smart, they can play around your abilities

What makes Zilean so special?

Zilean is a combo mage that provides massive utility to the team through offensive and defensive spell combinations. He is the only mage in the game with only a single damaging spell.

Zilean has the ability to do an AoE stun that last 1.5 seconds, a 99% slow that last for 2.5 seconds (or a 99% speed buff), and the ability to resurrect a champion that has taken fatal damage who will return with a moderate amount of health.

At the core, Support Zilean needs two things: enough mana to cast spells and enough cooldown reduction to quickly recast spells.

Many of the utility based items are cheap enough that so an optimal support build attained in a 30+ minute game. There are also options to build damage and be an AP threat later in the game.

By being able to control the tempo of battle through slows, stuns, and the occasional feigned death, Zilean is a powerful supportive champion.

The guides on this website focus on Zilean being the Support role, but many of the supportive ideologies can be carried over into the Mid and Top lane positions as well (the laning phase will be drastically different, but the general role within teamfighting is the same).

Runes

The Standard Support Mage Runepage

  • REDS

  • YELLOWS

  • BLUES

  • QUINTS

  • Magic Penetration7.8 MPen
  • Health72 HP
  • Ability Power10.7 AP
  • Armor (x2)8.5 Armor Ability Power4.9 AP

This is the most common page for support mages and this page works well into most matchups. The only matchups that it has trouble with are the heavy poke lanes, in which you'll want to take a full defensive page with minimal AP.

The magic penetration reds are the best fit for increasing the damage of Time Bomb through red runes.

The defensive seals and quints give you enough armor and HP to be resilient to poke in lane. The extra AP is great for poking out enemy ADCs and taking control of the lane.

For a more in-depth reasoning on why I choose these runes specifically, Runes 101 Guide.

The Defensive Runepage

  • REDS

  • YELLOWS

  • BLUES

  • QUINTS

  • Magic Penetration7.8 MPen
  • Health72 HP
  • Magic Resist12.1 MR
  • Armor12.8 Armor

This defensive page is one similar that you might run on a tankier champion (except taking MPen reds instead of Armor). You usually want to take this page when you know you are going into a rough lane (one with loads of poke) or if you are just starting out and need the extra defense to make it easier to recover from mistakes.

If you are just starting out and want to see some of the harder laning matchups where you may want to take this page, check out the Laning Matchups Guide.

Masteries

The Poke / AP Support Mastery Page
0/18/12

This mastery page is one of the more common support pages, but elects to take all 5 points into Meditation and 5 points into Intelligence over Precision. The extra 5% CDR is valuable to Zilean.

These masteries work well if you are learning Zilean or want to be an AP carry from the support position. You can build utility with these masteries, but it isn't as effective. For a detailed breakdown of each mastery talent, here's the Mastery Breakdown Guide.

The Ardent Censer Utility Page
0/12/18

This page uses the overwhelming amount of Zilean's CC to help heal and empower his team. With these masteries, you are wanting to build utility items and items with + to healing.

This build is turns on when you complete Ardent Censer, which will activate off of Stoneborn Pact's heal. This page is best if you plan on playing a pure support and taking advantage of Zilean's high amount of crowd control.

Skill Order

Level these skills in this order starting out:
  • Level 1Q
  • Level 2W
  • Level 3E

Now you get to choose which skill you max, either a damage focused early game or a CC focused early game. To see the pros and cons of going each build, check out The Great Q vs E Debate.

The Poke / AP Skill Order

This skill order focuses on harassing the enemy with damage and stuns, but sacrifices the utility of the debilitating slow. If you are wanting to carry the game without relying on your teammates as much, this is the recommended skill order. If you are just starting out, this is the skill order I recommend.

Max Order

  • ChronoshiftR
  • Time BombQ
  • RewindW
  • Time WarpE

For most players, Maxing Time Bomb on Zilean is the intuitive decision. It's his only damaging ability, the stun scales with levels, and it feels good when you land Double Bombs.

Most of the time, this is the skill order I go with unless I know I am playing with a skilled AD Carry.

The big negative I have with this build is that you WILL end up taking some CS from your Marksman when you go Double Bomb on the back line of the enemy creeps to land stuns onto enemies hiding behind minions. The Spells, Combos, and Tricks Guide explains several techniques that you can do with Double Bombs.

The Stoneborn Pact Skill Order

If you are planning on running Stoneborn Pact or want to have more control over the mid-game, then this is the build for you. This build focuses on having a bit of damage from Time Bomb during lane and then maxes the debilitating slow for the mid game. This skill order is used to enable your team and become a utility threat yourself, drawing resources away from killing your carries.

Max Order

  • Time Bomb3 x Q
  • ChronoshiftR
  • Time Warp Max E
  • Rewind Max W
  • Time BombFinish Max Q

Playing this skill order can be very difficult. At lower elos, team cohesion isn't as refined and players just tend to do whatever they want instead of making the best decisions for the team. At higher elos, this build works because it empowers your carries or severely inhibits enemies trying to kill your carries.

I recommend this skill order after having played quite a few games of Zilean or you have voice communication with your Marksman. The goal of this build is to empower your marksman and keep your carries safe, not necessarily blowing your enemy apart with bombs.

This build transitions well into a full utility item build set up.

Sample "Blow Stuff Up" Item Build

This is a damage build that works best with maxing Time Bomb (Q). Your focus on this build is getting CDR capped and building damage.
