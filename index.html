Why Play Zilean as a Support?

Do you enjoy:

  • Throwing clocks and bombs at people?
  • Playing a champion that tests the intelligence of your enemies?
  • Outplaying your opponents through clever use of skills?
  • Actively pushing buttons to cast spells with short cooldowns?
  • Playing a champion with different playstyles based on skill order?
  • Easily stopping teleport ganks?
  • Having outstanding tower dive offense and defense?
  • Zipping around the map at over 600 movement speed?
  • Being able to prolong, possibly even prevent death?

If you enjoy any or all of these things, I invite you to learn more so you can become the Zilgod and gain the admiration of your team as you carry them to victory, all as a support.

The road to becoming a Zilgod is not easy, and requires time and dedication.

Pros and Cons of Support Zilean

Pros

  • Scales well with levels, extremely strong ultity as game goes on
  • Excellent gank support through crowd control
  • Doesn't fall behind in levels later in the game
  • Low cost for optimal utility build
  • One of the most powerful Ultimates in the game
  • 33 second CD on Ultimate with Lvl 16 at 45% CDR

Cons

  • High mana costs for essential combos
  • Vulnerable to early all-ins pre level 6
  • Landing spell combos is tricky and easily dodged with mobility spells
  • Low mid and late game damage output
  • Easy to pick up, very difficult to master
  • If enemy is smart, they can avoid popping your Ultimate

What makes Zilean so special?

Zilean is a combo mage that provides massive utility to the team through offensive and defensive spell combinations. He is the only champion in the game with only a single damaging spell.

Zilean has the ability to do an AoE stun that last 1.5 seconds, a 99% slow that last for 2.5 seconds (or a 99% speed buff), and the ability to resurrect a champion that has taken fatal damage who will return with a moderate amount of health.

At the core, Support Zilean needs two things: enough mana to cast spells and enough cooldown reduction to quickly recast spells. Many of the utility based items are cheap enough that so an optimal support build attained in a 30+ minute game.

By being able to control the tempo of battle through slows, stuns, and the occasional feigned death, Zilean is a powerful supportive champion.

The guides on this website focus on Zilean being the Supprot role, but many of the supportive ideologies can be carried over into the Mid and Top lane positions as well (the laning phase will be drastically different, but the general role within teamfighting is the same).

This is the most common page for support mages and this page works well into most matchups. The only matchups that it has trouble with are the heavy poke lanes, in which you'll want to take a full defensive page.

The hybrid penetration reds are the best fit since you'll want to be harassing with auto attacks, as well with Time Bomb. This gives you the best value for offensive red runes.

The defensive seals and quints give you enough armor and HP to be resilient to poke in lane. The extra AP is great for poking out enemy ADCs and taking control of the lane.

For a more in-depth reasoning on why I choose these runes specifically, Runes 101 Guide.

The Standard Support Mastery Page

Out of all the masteries, picking up Thunderlord's is the best of all the trees, although Bond of Stone and Strength of Ages in the Resolve Tree are decent. For a detailed breakdown of each mastery talent, here's the Mastery Breakdown Guide.

This specific page is the standard page for most ranged supports in the bottom lane. It focuses on utility and survivability.

The Teamfight Utility Page
6/18/6

Many of the 18-point Keystone Masteries are terrible with Zilean support, but the most impactful masteries for Zilean are going to be the Mana Regen, Bandit for gold generation, the extra Cooldown Reduction (here's how the extra 5% makes a difference) and Expose Weakness for teamfighting.

Since Expose Weakness is a non-stacking buff (and an uncommon choice for most champions), this is an ideal choice for Zilean because it grants his team an extra 3% damage when a Time Bomb detonates. This page is more fragile during laning phase, but shines during the group fighting stage of the game.

Level these skills in this order starting out:

Now you get to choose which skill you max, either a damage focused early game or a CC focused early game. To see the pros and cons of going each build, check out The Great Q vs E Debate.

For most players, Maxing Time Bomb on Zilean is the intuitive decision. It's his only damaging ability, the stun scales with levels, and it feels good when you land Double Bombs.

Most of the time, this is the skill order I go with unless I know I am playing with a skilled AD Carry.

The big negative I have with this build is that you WILL end up taking some CS from your Marksman when you go Double Bomb on the back line of the enemy creeps to land stuns onto enemies hiding behind minions. The Spells, Combos, and Tricks Guide explains several techniques that you can do with Double Bombs.

The Utility CC Route

This skill order focuses on having an incredibly strong slow that synergizes with jungle ganks and speed boosts for low-mobility AD Carries. I recommend going this route after having played a few games of Zilean and having voice communication with your Marksman. You can also announce to your Marksman early on that you plan on maxing slow, and they should understand that your damage output will be low and they will have to do most of the work.

This skill order works amazingly with a High Plat or higher AD Carry, but may not be the best choice in Bronze or Silver (due to the higher mechanical skill needed in higher elo as an ADC).

The goal of this build is to provide your lane partner and yourself with the ability to have a huge mobility boost or a debilitating slow on a single opponent.

At level 5 when you have 3 ranks into Time Warp (E), you can give a 70% speed boost or a 70% slow. There are many critical uses for this skill and you can discover applications by Optimizing your Time Warp.

The items listed above are the most common buys for Support Zilean. I purposely avoid going to high ability power items and instead choose items with extra mana, cooldown reduction, and activatable items that provide more utility. Surprisingly, with this build you end up with about 250AP at 5 items while having plenty of team utility.

These items synergize well with Zilean, and I have a more detailed guide, "What to Build and When", which explains why these items are solid choices, as well as which items are "noob-traps".
