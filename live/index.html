Why Play Zilean as a Support?

Do you enjoy:
  • Button smashing like a boss?
  • Playing a high skill champion that isn't in the current support meta?
  • Throwing clocks at people?
  • Easily stopping teleport/TF ganks?
  • Having top-tier tower diving offense/defense?
  • Zipping around the map at over 600 movement speed?
  • Being able to prolong, possibly even prevent death?

If you enjoy any or all of these things, I invite you to learn more so you can become a Zilegod and gain the admiration of your team as you carry them to victory, all as a support.

Pros and Cons of Support Zilean

Pros

  • Scales well with levels, extremely strong late game
  • Moderate to Excellent gank support (depending on skill order)
  • Doesn't fall behind in levels later in the game
  • Low cost for optimal utility build
  • One of the most powerful Ultimates in the game
  • 33 second CD on Ultimate with Lvl 16 and max CDR

Cons

  • High mana costs for essential combos
  • Difficult laning phase (until 6)
  • Landing spell combos is tricky and easily dodged with mobility spells
  • Low late game damage output
  • If enemy is smart, they not pop your Ultimate

What makes Zilean so special?

Zilean is a caster that relies heavily on casting combinations of his spells which provide massive utility to the team through offensive and defensive spells; more so than being just a damage threat. He is the only champion in the game with only 1 damaging spell.

Zilean has the ability to do an AoE stun that last 1.5 seconds, a 99% slow that last for 2.5 seconds (or conversely a 99% speed buff), and the ability to resurrect a champion that has taken fatal damage and return it with a moderate amount of health.

At the core, Zilean needs enough mana to cast his spells, and enough cooldown reduction to quickly recast spells. Many of the utility based are cheap enough that an optimal full support build is easily attainable in a 30+ minute game.

By being able to control the tempo of battle through slows, stuns, and the occasional feigned death, Zilean is a powerful pure support champion.

The guides on this website focus on Zilean being the Supprot role, but many of the ideologies can be carried over into the Mid and Top lane positions as well (the laning phase will be drastically different, but the general role within teamfighting is the same).

Runes

My most used runepage
  • REDS
  • YELLOWS
  • BLUES
  • QUINTS
  • Attack Damage8.5 AD
  • Flat Health72 HP
  • Flat Mana Regen+3.0 Mana Regen / 5
  • Flat Armor13 Armor

This is the standard runepage that I prefer on Support Zilean. The two big notable stats are the AD reds and mana regen blues.

The flat reds are helpful in being a nuisance with auto-attacks in the laning phase. The chip damage can be enough to force an early potion or make the threat of a double bomb combo leading to a kill.

Zilean's early game revolves around being able to hold over 170 mana around level 3-4, which is when the first gank would arrive. The extra regen allows for some harass at levels 2 and 3 without being completely gassed out while you absorb gold for your Tear. Personally, I find the ability to have an extra speed boost or double bomb is enough to hold a gank, which is much more valuable than 12 Magic Resist.

For a more in-depth reasoning on why I choose these runes specifically, Runes 101 Guide.

Masteries

My most used mastery page

Let me preface this section by saying that all of the 18-point Keystone Masteries are terrible with Zilean support. The most impactful masteries for Zilean are going to be the Mana Regen and the extra Cooldown Reduction (to see how the extra 5% makes a difference, click here.)

Out of all the masteries, picking up Thunderlord's is the best of all the trees (although I'm experimenting with Bond of Stone in the Resolve Tree). To get a detailed breakdown of each mastery point, check out the Mastery Breakdown Guide.

For most players, Maxing Time Bomb on Zilean is the intuitive decision. It's his only damaging ability, the stun scales with levels, and it feels good when you land two bombs.

This is the build I usually pick into passive lanes (mostly Soraka lanes). This does fine in other situations, but the success of this build revolves around how well can you hit a double bomb combo.

The other big negative I have with this build is that you WILL take your lane's CS when you go for double bombs on the back line of the enemy creeps. The Spells, Combos, and Tricks Guide explains several techniques that you can do with double bombs.

These items synergize well with Zilean, and I have a more detailed guide, "What to Build and When", which explains why these items are solid choices, as well as which items are "noob-traps".
