Optimizing your Items; What to Buy and When

There are many different schools of thought on how to play the game and itemization, and these two builds are what I prefer to use.

As a utility support, your job is to keep your carries alive and out of danger, provide utility to the team through vision, and be dangerous enough to wield attention from the enemy team.

As an aggressive support, your job is to trade damage effectively, while being able to not let your laner fall behind in CS and health.

With that in mind, the best items that have worked for me are items that allow Zilean to spam out his spells without worrying about mana issues, have activatable items to empower your carries, and have mobility/escape to set up wards safely.

But sometimes it's fun to just blow people up. So we've got an item build for that too!

Just a forewarning, if you do focus damage output, there are certain downfalls to building mostly AP. Zilean has a very tough time damaging tanks, but can put some hefty damage down on squishier targets.

Recent Patch Changes

Patch 6.1 changed the build to be 5-slot efficient in the base. By adding the extra 10% CDR onto the upgraded Sightstone (Eye of the Oasis/Watchers), this means that you'll have 4 items giving CDR, 1 for tear, and then an open slot for pink wards or a defensive item.

This is huge because Zilean did not have an optimal 5-slot build path, so buying pink wards throughout the game was impossible unless you drastically change your build path.

Additionally, with the rise of the Boots of Swiftness, maxing E first isn't as devastating against ADCs, and so the "Blow Stuff Up" Build may be the better build at the moment.

What's the Big Deal about Zeke's Harbinger?

Zeke's Harbinger is an incredibly strong support item that grants 50% critical strike chance AND 20% Ability Power for 5 seconds when fully charged.

Zilean is because he is almost always casting spells with short cooldowns, which means a high uptime for Zeke's Harbinger.

1 charge is gained per second passively, and 4 are gained from an auto attack, and 8 from each spell cast. A common 4-spell combo nets 32 charges in 1 second.

Many of the current AD Carry builds crest at 50% critical chance with Inifinity Edge and Rapid Fire Cannon or Static Shiv. If they have ONLY these two crit items, then keep the Zeke's on them.

When your ADC has more than 50% crit, switch it over to an AP Carry. This grants an extra 20% AP, so at 400AP, they get a free Needlessly Large Rod (80AP).

At 600, they get an extra 120 AP.

A full build AP that has 790-820 AP (with flasks) would be pushed close to the 1000 AP mark, making them effectively a 6.5 item carry.

Mikael's Crucible vs. Banner of Command

When Mikael's Crucible is Better
  • When enemy team has multiple sources of CC or long duration CC
  • When money is more of an issue in the game
  • When your team has less map control
  • Generally, if your team is behind
  • A cheaper and quicker route to 45% CDR
  • MIKAEL'S DOES NOT STOP SUPPRESSION, ZED ULT, KNOCKUPS, OR BLINDS
When Banner of Command is Better
  • When your team needs extra MR or are facing multiple AP Threats
  • When Jungler didn't buy an Aegis of the Legion item
  • When you have a surplus of gold
  • When your team has better map control
  • When your team is ahead and has freedom to leave base
  • When 5-man pushing (Banner a Cannon in the side lane)

The 6th Item

  • Vision Wards75g
  • Zz'Rot Portal2700g
  • Zhonya's Hourglass3500g
  • Banshee's Veil2900g

As was mentioned before, the 6th item is crucial for a support. For the majority of the game, your 6th item slot SHOULD be reserved for pink wards.

In the previous builds, this was an issue because in order to reach 40% CDR AND have a large mana pool, all 6 items would be used. Now, you have freedom for your 6th pick.

When the game has gone on too long, you may want to pick up a defensive item. As with most defensive items, you should make your decision on what to take based on who is strong on the enemy team.

If they have strong initiation, take a Banshee's Veil. If they have heavy physical damage and burst, take a Zhonya's Hourglass. If you want raw resistances, you can use Zz'Rot Portal.

Which is Better: Grail or Morellonomicon?

When Athene's Unholy Grail is Better
  • When there are 2 or more sources of heavy magic damage
  • When you are having mana problems and need the extra regen from Chalice
  • When you NEED extra MR
  • The restoration of mana on kill is nice, but isn't too big of a factor
When Morellonomicon is Better
  • When you are against champions with plenty of sustain (Soraka, Mundo, Jhin)
  • When you want the most value for your gold (stat wise)
  • When you want to maximize the amount of AP you can squeeze in the build while being CDR capped
  • When money is more of an issue and you need items completed quicker

The 6th Item

  • Vision Wards75g
  • Zhonya's Hourglass3500g
  • Abyssal Scepter2350g
  • Banshee's Veil2900g
  • Rylai's Crystal Scepter3200g

As was mentioned before, the 6th item is crucial for a support. For the majority of the game, your 6th item slot SHOULD be reserved for pink wards.

Once you have all of your items completed, you can think about taking a final offensive/defensive item for your last slot.

Most of the time, you'll want to take the Hourglass. If you get caught out or have your ultimate on cooldown, this buys time for the team. It is also valuable against heavy AD teams.

Abyssal is the pick if they have AP Divers, such as Lissandra, Katarina, Diana, who will annihilate anyone without MR. Zilean doesn't gain any MR through levels, so he can get bursted down quickly. Banshee's is similar, but more focused on the defense rather than adding any more AP to the build.

Rylai's gives you a good chuck of AP and Health, but the slow mechanic isn't fantastic on Time Bomb. A 1 second slow is applied to all who are hit by the bomb after detonation, so it isn't that spectacular.

The Items to Avoid

  • Frost Queen's Claim2200g
    • Frost Queen's Claim has been nerfed numberous times since inception and is no longer the "go-to" item for AP casters.
    • The active "Spooky Ghosts" has been nerfed several times and has lost much of its utility.
    • If you can forego the active and extra 30AP, building into Eye of the Watchers is recommended because of its item slot efficiency.
  • Talisman of Ascension2200g
    • This used to be my "go-to" item before Eye of the Oasis had CDR, but now this guy is out.
    • Once again, this comes down to item slot efficiency vs. an extra activatible ability.
    • The active is somewhat wasted on Zilean because of the depreciation of movement speed past 490ms, and you can speed two people away at a higher speed.
  • Boots of Mobility800g
    • Boots of Mobility are useless on Zilean because every time you cast a spell onto an enemy (champion, creep, or monster), you get put in combat and drop to +25 Movespeed.
    • Another reason is that Zilean already has a huge speed buff increase in his kit, so you can roam quickly on Tier 2 Boots with Time Warp active.

  • Boots of Swiftness800g
    • I dislike taking these boots until the super late game and I can sell Boots of Lucidity and buy another item for an extra 10% CDR (either a Mikael's or Banner of Command.)
    • These boots aren't that bad, it's that you gain the extra movespeed and slow reduction at the cost of 10% CDR. So you have to ask yourself which is more important, more spells cast per fight, or being able to move 15 units faster per second?
