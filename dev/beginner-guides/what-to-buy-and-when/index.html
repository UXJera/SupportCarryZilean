Optimizing your Items

There are many different schools of thought on how to play the game and itemization, and this item build is what has worked best for me.

As a support, your job is to keep your carries alive and out of danger, provide utility to the team through vision, and be dangerous enough to weild attention from the enemy team.

With that in mind, the best items that have worked for me are items that allow Zilean to spam out his spells without worrying about mana issues, have activatible items to empower your carries, and have mobility/escape to set up wards safely.

There are other builds that want to focus towards damage output, but are not as effective for a support. Later in this guide I discuss some of the "alluring" items that work best for AP support mages, but not for Zilean.

Recent Patch Changes

Patch 6.1 changed the build to be 5-slot efficient in the base. By adding the extra 10% CDR onto the upgraded Sightstone (Eye of the Oasis), this means that you'll have 4 items giving CDR, 1 for tear, and then an open slot for pink wards or a defensive item.

This is huge because Zilean did not have an optimal 5-slot build path, so buying pink wards throughout the game was impossible unless you drastically change your build path.

The early Tear of the Goddess buy is one of the most important in the build. As the game progresses, this item effectively DOUBLES your mana pool. Zilean's basic spell combo starts at 155 mana per combo, and a full CC chain costs 295 mana (with max rank Q). With Tear and Zeke's, you have 2600 mana, along with the ridiculous regen from the Meditation talent.

The other items are standard, but you'll notice that we do not upgrade the trinket at all for quite some time in the build. The reason is that the more you invest into the trinket early game, the longer you put off Zeke's Herald.

What's the Big Deal about Zeke's Harbinger?

Zeke's Harbinger is an incredibly strong support item that grants 50% critical strike chance AND 20% Ability Power for 5 seconds when fully charged.

Zilean is because he is almost always casting spells with short cooldowns, which means a high uptime for Zeke's Harbinger.

1 charge is gained per second passively, and 4 are gained from an auto attack, and 8 from each spell cast. A common 4-spell combo nets 32 charges in 1 second.

Many of the current AD Carry builds crest at 50% critical chance with Inifinity Edge and Rapid Fire Cannon or Static Shiv. If they have ONLY these two crit items, then keep the Zeke's on them.

If the ADC has more than 50% crit, switch it to an AP Carry. This grants an extra 20% AP, so at 400AP, they get a free Needlessly Large Rod (80AP).

At 600, they get an extra 120 AP.

A full build AP that has 790-820 AP (with flasks) would be pushed close to the 1000 AP mark, making them effectively a 6.5 item carry.

From here, you should choose either Banner of Command or Mikael's Crucible, depending on the enemy's team composition. There are some points below that talk about when you should take one vs. the other, but this is a reactionary buy.

Upgrading your Tear should be a low priority, and you should try to straight up buy the final item, rather than holding onto a Needlessly Large Rod. The reason is that you lose the 6th slot efficiency that allows you to hold onto pink wards. If your pink ward is still alive and in a good position, feel free to pick up the NLR and upgrade as soon as possible.

Mikael's Crucible vs. Banner of Command

When Mikael's Crucible is Better
  • When enemy team has multiple sources of CC or long duration CC
  • When money is more of an issue in the game
  • Lanes are pushing AGAINST you
  • Generally, if your team is behind
  • Quicker route to 45% CDR
  • MIKAEL'S DOES NOT STOP SUPPRESSION, ZED ULT, KNOCKUPS, OR BLINDS
When Banner of Command is Better
  • When your team needs extra MR
  • When Jungler didn't buy an Aegis of the Legion item
  • When you have a surplus of gold
  • Lanes are pushing AWAY from you
  • When your team is ahead and has freedom to leave base
  • When 5-man pushing (Banner a Cannon in the side lane)

The 6th Item

  • Vision Wards75g
  • Zz'Rot Portal2700g
  • Zhonya's Hourglass3500g
  • Banshee's Veil2900g

As was mentioned before, the 6th item is crucial for a support. For the majority of the game, your 6th item slot SHOULD be reserved for pink wards.

In the previous builds, this was an issue because in order to reach 40% CDR AND have a large mana pool, all 6 items would be used. Now, you have freedom for your 6th pick.

When the game has gone on too long, you may want to pick up a defensive item. As with most defensive items, you should make your decision on what to take based on who is strong on the enemy team.

If they have strong initiation, take a Banshee's Veil. If they have heavy physical damage and burst, take a Zhonya's Hourglass. If you want raw resistances, you can use Zz'Rot Portal.

The Items to Avoid

  • Spellthief's Edge350g
    • Coin is 48 gpm (4 per creep x 6 creeps x 2 waves) plus 30 life per wave.
    • Spellthief is 8 per hit (max of 48 per min) + 10 extra damage (max 60 per min) and 5 AP.
    • For Spellthief's to work, you have to put yourself out to harass, making it predictable when your Spellthief is fully stacked.
    • If you don't land Time Bombs or autos, you lose out on gold efficiency.
  • Frost Queen's Claim2200g
    • Ancient Coin can be upgraded much later than Frost Queen's, while Frost Queen's demands a high finish priority, thus delaying the rest of your build significantly.
    • The active "Spooky Ghosts" seem like a good idea on Zilean, but he can speed himself up and get a stun combo off.
    • The active from Talisman is better for engagement/disengage, especially with the Captain Boots enchantment.
  • Talisman of Ascension2200g
    • I used to really like this item before Eye of the Oasis had CDR, but now this guy is out.
    • The active is somewhat wasted on Zilean because of the depreciation of movement speed past 490ms, and you can speed two people away at a higher speed.
    • Taking this means you won't have 5-slot efficency unless you pick up a 20% CDR item, which takes away from the utility of the build.
  • Athene's Unholy Grail2800g
    • You want to buy this ONLY if you are trying to achieve 40% CDR with only 3 items.
    • Athene's gives 20% CDR, which means you give up 2: CDR Boots, Zeke's or Mikaels (Ancient Coin upgrades should NEVER be omitted from the build).
    • The mana regen from the kill/assist passive isn't that great because you have enough regen full build.
  • Morellonomicon2550g
    • This is similar theme to Athene's, except offers no defensive stats, but with easier components.
    • If someone on the team needs to get the Grievous Wounds buff, it shouldn't be Zilean, his damage isn't consistent enough to get the proc when it's absolutely needed.
    • Even with 400+ AP, you still won't be able to burst through tanks for the debuff.
  • Boots of Mobility800g
    • Zilean has insane movespeed with Time Warp, and Movespeed is scalar so you don't benefit as much.
    • Dropping down to a base of 350 movespeed is really bad because you can get chain slowed.
  • Boots of Swiftness800g
    • These are similar to Boots of Mobility, but with fewer drawbacks.
    • You have to ignore the "Swifties Fever" (buying Boots of Swiftness on every champion) because it does not make a significant speed increase while his Time Warp is on himself.
    • Casting Time Warp on yourself will override the movespeed loss of most spells, making these not as effective as other champions.

    • Also, you'll have to find another 10% CDR that these guys don't bring.
  • Items built from Needlessly Large Rod3000+ gold
    • Items that build out of Needlessly Large Rod are incredibly expensive
    • Inventory space is tight within this build and there isn't space to spare for components.
    • AP shouldn't be a focus for Support Zilean, and ultility should be priority 1
    • Archangel's Staff is the ONLY NLR item to go for as a damage buffer with it's active.
    • NLR items should only be built on Mid Zilean OR for the final item if you are building offensive.
