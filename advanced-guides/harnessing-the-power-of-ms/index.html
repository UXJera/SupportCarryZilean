Harnessing the Power of Movespeed

Movement Speed is one of the more mysterious quantities in League of Legends, and most view the stat as an arbitrary number of "how fast you go" and a relative measure between two champions.

In reality, Movement Speed is the number of units you travel per second. For instance, a your standard ADC moves 325 units per second without boots, and most supports move at 335 units per second.

Movement Speed FAQ

  • If my current movement speed is 390, and I get a buff of 99%, why isn'y my movement speed 799 (390 * 1.99)?
  • Since movement speed buffs are stackable, Riot implemented scalar limitations so that movement speed buffs don't get out of control.
  • The 99% slow doesn't stop them, but only drops their movement speed to 112, even though 99% of 390 is 39. Why are these numbers not the same?
  • Similar to how movement speed increases work, slows are also scaled at a certain point. Since slows are no longer multiplicative, the theoretical minimum of slow caps at 110 movement speed.
  • If I'm being chased, should I speed myself or should I slow them?
  • Most cases, it's better to speed yourself up because you are guaranteed 2.5 seconds of movement speed, versus a possible slow reduction if the enemy has any Tenacity or Slow Resistance in their masteries. Also, if there are multiple enemies in the area, you can only slow 2 of them instantly(with 35% CDR), so speeding yourself up is safer.
    It is better to slow the enemy if you have teammates close by who can punish the overextend.
  • How do Mercury's Treads and Boots of Swiftness affect being slowed by Zilean?
  • The Mercury's Treads reduce the duration of the slow, but not it's potency. This means that Zilean's slow goes from 2.5 seconds to 2 seconds.
    Boots of Swiftness still have the same duration, but the potency of the slow is greatly reduced. For example, a Rank 5 Time Warp only slows down to 162 movement speed, when it would normally reduce it to 112.

Movement speed is calculated by a long formula, which is shown below.

  • [Base Movement Speed + Flat Movement Speed Bonuses (boots)] *
  • [1 + Additional Percentage Movement Bonuses] *
  • [1 + First Multiplicative Percentage Movement Bonus (Zilean Time Warp)] *
  • [1 + Any Further Multiplicative Percentage Movement Bonuses] *
  • [1 - Highest Slow Ratio * Slow Resist Ratio]

Another formula to note is that there are three movement speed "caps", two speed ups and one slow down.

The first movement speed cap is between 415 and 490 movement speed, where it is only 80% effective. That means you spend 90 Movement Speed to gain 75.

The second cap is everything over 490, and that cap you only get 50% efficiency. This is the reason why Zilean caps out around 620 movement speed, while the base math should be closer to 780.

The big take away is once your movement speed is over 490, its scaling is halved.

Slow is a very powerful tool, and even though the illusion of 99% movement speed reduction would drop mobility to next to 0, it isn't quite that way.

The third movement speed cap is on slows that reduce mobility below 220, from there the movement speed is halved.

Since stacking slow was taken out of the game, the maximum slow ratio is 99%, the movement speed floor is 112, which is still a debilitating slow.

Slow Resist has a huge impact on the effect of the slow, especially at high ranks of Time Warp.

In general, you want to avoid casting Time Warp on enemies with Swifties because it makes your spell at least 1 rank less effective. However, if you are being disengaged upon or find a pick, ALWAYS use Time Warp no matter what. The worst case scenario is that you get the enemy to burn a Flash.
