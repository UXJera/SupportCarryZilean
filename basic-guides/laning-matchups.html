Laning Matchups

Laning is one of the toughest aspects when learning Support Zilean. There are two main playstyles: reactive and aggressive, and both depend on how you decide to skill your ranks.

Laning Parity

Laning parity is the mirroring of the type of support you are playing against. If you are in a lane that has lots of poke, you want to max Time Bomb over Time Warp. If you are playing against a lane that wants to all-in, you want to max Time Warp over Time Bomb.

For example, if you are against an Alistar, you want to max Time Warp so if he gets out of position, your marksman can poke him down (ultimately stopping the all-in because he is afraid of dying in lane).

An example of when to max Time Bomb is when you are against a poke lane or a sustain lane, such as Soraka or Janna. By throwing out more damage, you have a stronger trades, force the support to expend more mana, and can dish out some strong poke that may force one of them to leave lane early.

Laning Mindset: Reactive

When you are maxing Time Warp, Zilean's lane phase is his weakest point in the game. If you fall too far behind, you can ruin your ability to control the game.

The best mindset coming into lane is "Survive the Lane, Win the Game". It is OK to play passive and react to plays, as long as you aren't dying or losing a large amount of CS. Zilean lanes will always have the XP advantage if both lanes are equal in time spend in lane.

Laning Mindset: Aggressive

When you are maxing Time Bomb, you are looking to land as many bombs on the enemy as possible, while still being able to avoid incoming damage from spells, autos, and minions.

By lowering the HP of your opponent, you force them to play more cautiously and have less pressure in lane, and should force the hand of the jungler. Zilean has plenty to deal with junglers, so other lanes can get further ahead and have knowledge of where the enemy jungle should be focusing.

Laning Power Spikes

Level 2: This is when you can first use your Double Bomb. If you push in the lane and hit 2 first, you can do some serious damage and force out a potion from the enemy ADC. This should cause that player to play more defensive.

Level 5: If you are maxing Time Bomb, this is when your Time Bomb gets a huge spike in damage (from 115 dmg up to 165). At the early stages of the game, this is some serious damage with 2 Bombs going off.
If you are maxing Time Warp, you get a 70% slow on the enemy. This drops the enemy movement to half of what your ADC is at, which means you will be able to get off extra autos, or force a flash. Do this especially if the enemy support roams after the 1st buy.

Level 6: This is when your ultimate is unlocked, and is a huge defensive tool. This enables solid dives, or gives your ADC the chance to be fearless and go all-in on the enemy ADC without fear of death. Zilean's ultimate has a duration of 5 seconds, which gives more than enough time to deal some extreme damage (or escape).

What does and doesn't lane well with Support Zilean

Zilean has an interesting kit which allows him to play two distinct playstyles depending on the match-up: A heavy poke damage lane, or a heavy crowd control lane where you usually have an extra member to follow up on damage.

Best Marksmen to pair with Zilean

These champions are the best pairing with Zilean for two factors:

  1. 1) Marksmen who lack mobility
  2. 2) Marksmen that can root or follow up to crowd control

Many of these champions have a distinct weakness or flaw, and Zilean can help shield against that weakness. For example, Vayne needs help with positioning and is prone to all-in dives. But by having Zilean ult, you can make them waste spells trying to kill the Marksman as the team cleans up the tanks.

Generally, maxing Time Warp is more favorable than Time Bomb, but you want to make that decision based on what lane you are going against.

Decent Marksmen to pair with Zilean

This group is the majority of Marksmen, who all work well with Zilean. While there are some that excel with Zilean's kit, most benefit from the speed up and protection with Zilean's ult.

Maxing Time Bomb is more favorable with this group, but maxing Time Warp is fine.

Poor Marksmen to pair with Zilean

Ezreal is the only marksman that comes to mind who doesn't synergize well with Zilean in one form or another. Ezreal has plenty of mobility and great poke damage, but his burst within a 2 second combo is very low.

If you do lane with a Zilean, you want to max Time Bomb and build AP, because you're going to try poking as hard as possible.

Enemy Marksmen Matchups

For the most part, the enemy support has a much larger impact on the lane than the AD Carry.

There are some rough matchups for Zilean (those with dashes), and some good matches (those without dashes).

AD Carries that do a massive amount of burst damage are dangerous to Zilean.

Easiest Marksmen for Zilean to face

Similarly, the champions that work best with Zilean are also countered by Zilean. These champions have low mobility and very predictable playstyles. Against these champions, it's recommended that you max Time Warp because they have low damage in lane and their mispositioning can be exploited.

Average Difficulty Marksmen for Zilean to face

These are the standard champions that Zilean does OK against. Against these champions, it's important to know the limitations of the enemy support and cater your playstyle against that.

Many of these champions have predictable burst points, and if you are playing correctly, you can identify when they are moving in for the kill (usually by advancing into a minion wave as you are retreating) and prepare to use your ultimate or throw bombs to zone if you aren't level 6 yet.

Challenging Marksmen for Zilean to face

These are the marksmen with high mobility and high burst or poke and can destroy the lane if you get out of position slightly. These are the champions that can crush you in lane if you don't play intelligently.

Ezreal has plenty of mobility and range, so landing Time Bombs without taking poke is very difficult.

Sivir is the worst ADC matchup to play against because she has two chances to absorb the spell, either as it lands, or as it detonates. This makes it incredibly difficult to play around her spellshield.

Many of the 4-star marksmen have incredible pre-6 damage and can easily zone you out (or engage for kills) if you are out of position. The enemy support will be a huge factor on if you should take a passive or aggressive playstyle against these marksmen.

Enemy Support Matchups

Zilean's greatest threat is the enemy support. There are so many unfavorable matchups that you have to play wisely.

In general, All-In supports and heavy poke supports are strong against Zilean, while melee tanks are weak.

You should decide if you are maxing Q or E based on the enemy support (not the AD Carry).

Instead of ordering by the difficulty (which changes every patch), here's the list alphabetically.

Alistar

If you slow the Alistar when he tries to engage or leave, he looks like a goof and your ADC can punish him heavily. Having an mobility edge punishes Alistar hard.

Annie

Annie used to be a scary burst mage, but the changes lowered her base damage, forcing her to build more AP to be effective. Tibbers does a majority of the damage, so don't stand near your ADC if this is your opponent.

Bard

Bard is annoying with his sustain and poke/CC. When you see him walk away from lane to pick up charms, pressure the enemy ADC because of the 2v1.

Blitzcrank

Blitzcrank is always tough if you can't dodge hooks. A strategy is to Double Bomb the enemy ADC if someone gets grabbed. You can also slow the Blitz if he walks up and misses the hook and then punish.

Brand

Brand was changed in patch 6.9 and isn't that scary in lane. If he gets ahead, then he becomes problematic when grouping. Support Brand has low gold economy and needs AP to make an impact in teamfights.

Braum

Braum's wall can stop Time Bombs from going past him, but they auto sticky to him, so you get an easy stun every time. You have to be careful if he jumps forward to a minion to land auto attacks, but overall he isn't that difficult of a support.

Fiddlesticks

The key to dealing with Fiddlesticks is standing far enough apart from your laner so that the crows don't bounce between eachother. Other than that, it is very easy to negate a Fiddle by slowing or stunning him.

Galio

Galio can be tricky, since he does have high magic resist and his spells do hurt. Galio has a shallow mana pool and runs out quickly, so laning isn't difficult. Watch out for his Flash/Ult at 6 with a jungler.

Janna

Against Janna, you want to max Time Bomb and try to hit both champions or Double Bomb whoever isn't shielded. Janna forces the lane into passivity and has plenty of mobility, meaning it is wiser to not waste resources unless you have a gank coming.

Karma

Karma is one of the hardest matchups for Zilean. Her shields negate the damage of Time Bomb, her speed up is on par with low rank Time Warp, and her poke is extremely painful to deal with. You need to max bomb to keep up with her damage output and not fall too far behind.

Kennen

Kennen is an uncommon pick, but has incredibly predictable playstyle. Just slow him when he goes in to Lightning Rush and ultimate. Kennen has to be pretty close to be a threat, so you can punish him with CC for coming too close.

Leona

Leona is the one of the hardest melee matchups for Support Zilean. Try to Double Bomb the enemy Marksman when she dives in, and try to poke her as much as possible in lane. You may want to play passive into this lane.

Lulu

Lulu's kit was changed to favor maxing her shields, rather than her poke damage. Lulu's Glitterlance now has a 2 second decaying slow at rank 1, which means that her ADC will be the one pumping out the damage.

Lux

Lux is an annoying poke champ, but nothing special. Her shield is much weaker than your bombs, so it's better to deal more damage than have the better slow.

Malphite

Malphite is another uncommon matchup who basically sits around in lane and waits until 6. Against a support Malphite, play incredibly agressive because he cannot punish.

Morgana

Morgana can shield your Double Bomb stun reliably, but the slow has no cast time and can be used before she gets Black Shield off. Much like Blitzcrank, if you can dodge her skillshot, you should be fine.

Nami

Nami is the toughest of the sustain lanes. Her poke, CC, and healing are incredibly strong. Poke down enemies to make her waste mana on heals, and do your best to dodge the bubble. It is difficult to play agressively, so you may be forced to play passively or reactively.

Nautilus

Nautilus has fallen out of favor, but he's strong as a support with the CC from his kit. Once Nautilus goes in, it's hard for him to retreat, so you can get heavy damage onto him. He does tend to snowball if he gets an advantage.

Poppy

Poppy has fallen out of favor, but is a strong mobile support. You have to be careful to not get too close to a wall. Slowing Poppy makes it easy to get some quick damage onto her.

Shen

If played properly, Shen does a massive of damage. If you haven't played against one, you need to know that if you get taunted at level 2, you will probably die. Support Shen is somewhat fragile, so you can poke him out over time.

Sona

Sion can be a bit tricky. Normally, support Sion's max the minion knockback move, then walk up and do the knock up. He does a deceptive amount of damage and is extremely durable. Slowing Sion works out well.

Sona

Sona's poke and sustain hurt, so just kill her quick with an all-in or consistent poke. She's an easy kill with levels in Q at levels 2, 4 and 5 if she's out of position.

Soraka

Soraka is commonly banned, but she isn't too much of a problem for Zilean. If you can land damage onto her, it works out well. With the changes to Soraka, she'll be winning most of the trades if she's smart and is maxing her Q. You will likely be forced into a passive lane if she maxes Heal first.

Syndra

Syndra is a tough support for Zilean because her ranged stun does plenty of damage and a long duration stun. You must pay attention to her ball placement and always be aware of her angle so she can't do her stun.

Tahm Kench

Tahm Kench is a huge pain who can negate Double Bomb damage by eating his ally. Your best bet to winning this lane is by maxing Time Warp and having your marksman whittle him down.

Taric

After the rework, Taric is strong but he is predictable. Taric has mana problems, which means if you can poke down the enemy, Taric will have a hard time. Taric's ult is strong for dives, but Zilean can negate one death.

Thresh

This is a common and tough matchup into Zilean. Dodge hooks and Double Bomb the marksman is the strategy. This is a skill matchup, so you may have trouble if you aren't familiar laning against Thresh.

Trundle

All you have to do against Trundle is slow him when he tries to pillar you off. He has good sustain, so you can't really poke him out, so it is better all-in to get the kill with the help of the jungler.

Veigar

Veigar can get out of control if he lands enough skills on you. This matchup isn't difficult if you avoid his spells. His ultimate will kill you or your laner if below 30% HP, so be ready to Chronoshift if things go poorly.

Vel'Koz

Vel'Koz's damage is weighted towards landing 3 abilities to do more damage, and his individual spell poke was taken down quite a bit. Vel'Koz will try to play far back, so it is difficult to land Double Bombs.

Zyra

Zyra is a huge pain in lane. Her damage and harass is high, and has the range advantage to win most trades against you. You may be forced to play passively against this lane in order to survive.
