Mastery Breakdown

Season 7 preface: As in Season 6, there is no 18-point keystone mastery that synergizes perfectly with Zilean. In fact, there aren't many changes from the Season 6 mastery list to Season 7.

Despite a lackluster selection for keystone talents, there are several minor talents in the Cunning Tree that work well with Zilean, so Cunning Tree is the most optimal mastery path.

There are only a few "mandatory" talents that I recommend, such as Meditation, Bandit, and Intelligence. The other talents are simply the best of the alternative choices.

0/18/12 - Extra Health and Summoner Cooldown

This is the standard support page for most mage supports. It offers a bit of defense, and plenty of utility. The difference between this page and the 6/18/6 page is that you have more room to make mistakes during lane with this build.

0/18/12 will likely be the go-to page for Support Zilean, with some minor adjustments in the Tenacity tree depending on how aggressive your opposing lane will be.

6/18/6 - Teamfight Utility

This runepage is similar to the Mid Lane Zilean page, with some slight differences. This page gives you the most offensive value at the cost of defense and health regeneration.

This runepage also takes Expose Weakness, which does grant some teamfight utility. The largest difference between this mastery tree and the previous is the scaling %-based damage while in combat. Zilean's Time Bomb automatically puts him into combat when it lands, which means that you will be getting a 3% damage on Time Bomb overall. Combined with enough AP, this can be a potent combination without the drawback from Double Edged Sword.

This is the page you want to use when you want to go glass cannon and all-out damage. You must be very cautious with your positioning and aware of enemy roams and ganks.

Ferocity Talent Tree

The Feroicity Tree is an offensive based tree that works better with higher damage item builds from Mid Zilean and doesn't have as much power with Support Zilean.
I do not recommend taking any points in the Ferocity tree, but I will go through and show the strengths of each in case you want to do some experimenting.

  • Fury

    +4% Attack Speed

  • Sorcery

    +2% increased Ability damage

The extra attack speed doesn't benefit Support Zilean at all, so take the extra 2% damage from abilities. This is more beneficial for an AP mid Zilean, but can work well for a Support Zilean, especially if you are focusing more on AP and maxing Time Bomb early.

  • Fresh Blood

    Basic attacks vs enemy champions deal (11 + 1 per champion level) bonus damage (9 sec CD per target)

  • Feast

    Killing a unit restores 20 Health (30 second cooldown)

  • Expose Weakness

    Your damaging abilities debuff enemies for 3 seconds, increasing the damage they take from allied champions by 3%

Fresh Blood is a solid talent for early game if you plan on playing aggressively vs your lane opponents. This skill does fall off after laning phase, but can pack a wallop early on.

Expose Weakness is the best choice of the three because Double Bombs will give about 6 seconds of the debuff (3 for when the initial bomb goes, 3 more after the second). This is weaker than Fresh Blood during the early game but is much more powerful when grouped.

The extra 3% damage should be fairly constant since you spam Time Bomb for zoning and stuns in teamfights.

It should also be noted that Expose Weakness does not stack if other allies are using Expose Weakness. However, many other laners prefer Feast or Double Edged Sword to be stronger in lane.

Feast isn't good for supports, period.

  • Vampirism

    +2% Lifesteal and Spell Vamp

    ONLY 33% EFFECTIVE

  • Natural Talent

    +10 Attack Damage and 15 Ability Power at level 18

    (.55 Attack Damage and .83 Ability Power per level)

The spell vamp isn't useful for Zilean because it has reduced effect on AoE spells, so the AD/AP per level is better by default. An extra 15AP isn't a deal breaker late game, but it is far better than the alternative.

  • Bounty Hunter

    Deal 1.5% increased damage for each unique enemy champion you have killed

  • Double Edged Sword

    Deal 5% increased damage but take 2.5% more damage from all sources

  • Battle Trance

    Deal 5% increased damage over 5 seconds when in combat with enemy champions

Of the three, Battle Trance is easily the best for Support Zilean for damage. When your Time Bomb lands on a champion, you are put into combat for a minimum of 3 seconds automatically, so it is very easy to reach the max value for this effect.

The other two options are too conditional or have too high of a risk associated. Even for mid lane, Battle Trance is the best pick.

I do not recommend going this far into the tree because the final keystones are poor choices for Zilean.

  • Battering Blows

    7% Armor Penetration

  • Piercing Thoughts

    7% Magic Penetration

For this tree, the Magic Penetration is better than the Armor Penetration, but the flat penetration in Cunning is a better option overall.

  • Warlord's Bloodlust

    Basic attacks gain increasingly more life steal based on your missing health

    Zilean shouldn't rely on auto attacks for sustain, so do not take this ever.

  • Fervor of Battle

    When you deal damage to an enemy, you gain a stacking buff that causes auto attacks to deal bonus damage at max stacks

    Zilean shouldn't be getting enough auto attacks to reliably use this talent, so this is pointless.

  • Deathfire Touch

    Your spells cause enemy champions to take magic damage equal to 20% of your Ability Power, halved for Area of Effect spells

    Of all the keystones in this tree, this is the best choice, although it isn't a very good one.

    Since Time Bomb is your only damaging spell and deals 50% less Deathfire Touch damage, it is better to just go the Thunderlord's Decree route and get either of the better Tier 3 talents in that tree.

Cunning Talent Tree

The Cunning Tree is the utility based tree, which has several powerful talents to aid the Zilean player.

  • Wanderer

    3% Movement Speed out of combat

  • Savagery

    Single attacks and spells deal 5 bonus damage to minions

The extra 3% movespeed out of combat is a solid choice for early game. Once you have Time Warp maxed out, this becomes less important, but it is still the better alternative than Feast.

The extra damage to minions in lane isn't worth taking since your ADC should be getting all of the farm anyways. You could take this if you plan on shoving lanes quickly or are laning w/ a Jhin and need to help him farm.

  • Runic Affinity

    Jungle Buffs last 15% longer

    18 second longer on blue/red buff

    27 seconds longer on Baron buff

  • Secret Stash

    Potions last 10% longer (+15 heal)

    Potions are switched with Biscuits that restore 15 Health and 15 Mana on use

  • Assassin

    Deal 2% increased damage against champions when no allied champions are nearby

Having the stronger potions is the best option in lane because it nets and extra 30 health that helps you sustain through the rough early game.

Having the extra duration on the buff is better for a mid-laner, although I'd probably still take the stronger potions for mid-lane.

Assassin is useless because you shouldn't be dueling anyone to begin with.

  • Merciless

    Deal 5% increased damage to champions below 40% health

  • Meditation

    Regenerate 1.5% of your missing Mana every 5 seconds

The mana regen is probably the best talent to take a Support Zilean in the entire mastery list. When you build more mana, you get an even greater effect from this talent. This is huge when you buy a Tear of the Goddess early, which solves most of Zilean's mana problems.

Near the end of the game, your mana pool will be well over 2000 mana, and even at half mana, you'll be getting at least 15 mana per 5, and even more the lower your mana is. This helps Zilean use his bombs for scouting and zoning, and be liberal with the use of Time Warp.

The extra damage just isn't as valuable with Zilean's kit.

  • Greenfather's Gift

    Stepping into brush causes your next damaging attack or ability to deal 3% of your target's current health as bonus magic damage (9s cooldown)

  • Bandit

    Gain 1 gold for each nearby minion killed by an ally.

    Gain 3 gold when hitting an enemy champion with a basic attack (5s cooldown)

  • Dangerous Game

    Champion kills and assists restore 5% of your missing Health and Mana

As a support, the default go-to is Bandit because it increases the passive gold generation and rewards for harassing in lane.

Greenfather's Gift works best if you are playing against a passive lane and can gain brush control. The downside is that you'll be losing passive gold trading auto attack damage for extra gold. In my opion don't believe the loss is gold is worth the extra auto attack damage.

Dangerous Game is the worst of the 3 choices and should be avoided.

  • Precision

    Gain Lethality (8.5) and Magic Penetration per level

  • Intelligence

    Your Cooldown Reduction cap is increased to 45% and you gain 5% Cooldown Reduction

The 5% cap increase for CDR is one of the best talents because it provides the highest value for Zilean in terms of spell rotations. To see just how much of a difference the extra 5% makes throughout the game, visit the CDR and You Guide.

Precision is an alternative to the Ferocity's percentage based Armor/MR reduction. For most champions, this would be the choice talent, but for Support Zilean, the extra CDR is more valuable than the extra damage per spell.

  • Stormraider's Surge

    Dealing 30% of a champion's max Health within 2 seconds grants 40% Movement Speed and 75% slow resist for 3 seconds

    As Support Zilean, you won't reliably have the chance to burst out an enemy unless you are building AP early and maxing Time Bomb. This is a much better choice for the mid lane.

  • Thunderlord's Decree

    Your 3rd attack or spell on an enemy champion deals extra damage.

    Out of all of the keystone masteries, this is the most useful of the bunch. Landing 3 consecutive spells/attacks adds a minor amount of damage.

    This is most useful in laning when you can land a Double Bomb combo and throw an auto attack to proc the Thunderlords.

    As the game progresses, this mastert becomes weaker and weaker (due to AP being limited and mis-positioning being less forgiving).

  • Windspeaker's Blessing

    Your heals are 10% stronger
    Heals on other allies increase their armor and magic resist by a flat amount for 3 seconds

    If this mastery was changed to give the bonus to ANY buff given (or they just included movespeed bonus), this would be the best choice.

    Currently, the only spell that this procs off of is the Chronoshift revival, which offers no early game bonus, and a limited effect as the game goes on.

Resolve Talent Tree

The Resolve Tree is the defensive tree that offers solid secondary choices for laning.

  • Recovery

    +2 Health per 5 seconds

  • Unyeilding

    +5% Bonus Armor and Magic Resist

The health regen is better for Zilean in lane because as you auto attack the enemy, the minions WILL attack you back and deal a considerable amount of damage. This helps you bounce back from that deficit.

The extra Armor/MR isn't worth it because Zilean typically builds only 1 moderate resist item of each type. I do recommend picking up a Zeke's Harbinger and an item that builds out of Chalice, the extra 5% adds up to an extra 1-2 Armor and MR.

  • Explorer

    +15 Movement Speet in Brush and River

  • Tough Skin

    You take 2 less damage from champion basic attacks

  • Siegemaster

    Gain 8 Armor and MR when near an allied tower

These 3 choices can be changed depending on what matchup you are playing into. Planning to roam more? Take Explorer. Want to play aggressive? Take Tough Skin. Planning to have a rough time in lane? Take Siegemaster.

Generally, I prefer the extra MS in the river and brush because it makes escapes much easier. This movement speed is added onto the base movement speed, so you get a higher scaling Time Warp in the river. I like the utility of this talent over the minuscule damage reduction.

  • Runic Armor

    Shields, healing, regeneration, and lifesteal on you are 8% stronger

  • Veteran's Scars

    +50 Health

The extra 50HP is the best option here. This is an early game talent which equates to 6 HP Seals or 2 HP Quints, so it is a substantial amount of health. If you are running HP seals or quints, this will put you over 600HP going to lane, which is good for learning and facing aggressive lanes.

The only benefit from the Runic Armor is the extra 8% increase to your regeneration and health from Ancient Coin, if you choose to go that item. This is such a small amount that it isn't worth it. You gain an extra 0.1 hp regen per 2 seconds in lane and a 0.9 hp regen when using a potion.

  • Insight

    Reduce the cooldown of Summoner Spells by 15%

    Flash/Teleport | 300sec -> 255sec
    Heal | 240sec -> 204sec
    Ignite/Exhaust | 210sec -> 180sec

  • Perseverance

    +50% Base Health Regen, increased to +200% when below 25% Health

  • Fearless

    Gain a bonus Armor and MR for 2 seconds when damaged by enemy champions

The lower cooldown on Summoner Spells is the most common choice in this tree, for good reason. For example, if you are tracking your opponents summoner spells by their default values (see chart above), you may be off by 45 seconds on a Flash timing (255s vs 300s Flash). Also having these spells more readily available makes more opportunities to engage/prevent plays.

The extra health regen is not that useful. Since Support Zilean has decent sustain if you go with the Ancient Coin and three potions, this mastery is a bit unnecessary for him.

Fearless is not a worthwhile talent to take for Support Zilean because he should not be tanking and does not have enough base tank stats to make this talent useful.

  • Swiftness

    +15% Tenacity and Slow Resist

  • Legendary Guardian

    +3 Armor and Magic Resist for each nearby enemy champion

The extra tenacity is a nice thought, but Zilean is supposed to be elusive and able to run away before those hard CC spells land. Often this is useful in the early game while Zilean gets the levels and items needed to leave an impact in the game.

The bonus to defenses when enemies are around is not a good thing for Zilean, as he shouldn't be anywhere near the enemy team's core. At most, you will have 3 people diving in, which only grants you 9 armor/mr in a position where you shouldn't be. Ultimately, this talent is best for champions who engage, and Zilean does best counter-engaging and creating picks.

  • Grasp of the Undying

    Every 4 seconds in combat your next attack against enemy champions deals and steals .75% of your max Health

    The auto attacks stealing 7-10 health seems alright, but the damage you take from the creeps attacking you back is not worth the small amount of life stolen. Plus you have to actively be in combat to use this, which turns off your passive for 10 seconds.

    In the late game, Zilean doesn't have a large HP pool and you shouldn't be able to get free auto attacks outside of picking off an enemy straggler.

  • Courage of the Colossus

    Gain a shield for 4 seconds after hitting an enemy champion with hard crowd control. This can apply multiple times if multiple enemies are hit. 30s cooldown

    This talent is great for initiating tanks, but isn't great for Zilean. Zilean does have an easy time landing mutli-member crowd controls, but most of the time there is movement away from the fight so the shield would quickly fall off before damage can be absorbed.

  • Bond of Stone

    +4% Damage Reduction.

    6% of the damage taken by the nearest allied champion is dealt to you instead

    This is the second best keystone talents for Support Zilean across trees, this is the second best option. You will always have a 4% damage reduction, and help out your lane partner by soaking some of his damage.

    Depending on how aggressive your enemy laners are, you may even want to take this over Thunderlord's and the extra CDR.
