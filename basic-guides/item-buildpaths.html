Item Buildpaths: Optimizing your Items

Right now, there are 2 thematic ways to build Zilean: Poke or Utility. Many of the support item changes now make it harder to mix and match while having a successful build. As of Patch 7.12, many of the item sets do not seem to work out well in high gold+ elo.

The first type of build is Poke, which takes Thunderlord's Mastery and looks to build damage throughout the game, and doesn't rely on the items to provide additional utility to the team. This build is better for carrying the game by yourself because you are a damage threat, but does not work well if you fall behind or if enemies build enough magic resist.

The second type of build is Utility, which takes Stoneborn Pact Mastery and looks to empower your attack speed carries through small heals which provide the buff. This build is better for team play and competent teammates, seeing as you will not be contributing much damage to teamfights and are acting as a crowd-control bot.

In general, when playing Zilean in Bronze-Gold, the more you have to rely on yourself to carry the game. In Plat-Diamond, empowering your team's carries are important. The reason is that it is harder to get the gold income to be overly powerful, and higher elo players are more knowledgeable about avoiding damage or mitigating damage.

Over the next few patches, this page will change from item build progression throughout the game and turn into "what items work best in these situations".

The Stoneborn Pact Healing Build

  • Eye of the Watchers
  • Boots of Lucidity
  • Ardent Censer
  • Redemption
  • Athene's Unholy Grail
  • Oracle Alteration

Stoneborn Pact is a mastery that works well with Zilean in certain conditions. It super charges attack speed carries and provides a unique way to play Zilean.

The focus of this build is to be a utility threat in fights, not relying on your own damage. You want to shield and heal your team by crowd controlling the enemies.

Starting Items

  • Spellthief's Edge350g
  • 3 Potions150g
  • Ward Trinket

Spellthief's Edge is the best choice for pressuring lane and the extra mana regeneration. Alternatively, you can use Ancient Coin, but you have less precense in lane. I've had good success with both of these items.

First Back and Early Game

  • Frostfang550g
  • 2 x Faerie Charm250g
  • Sightstone800g
  • Tier 1 Boots300g
  • Forbidden Idol550g

Your first back should be with around 500 - 700 gold. Your first purchase should be your upgraded starting item. Frostfang offers extra poke damage and mana regen, Nomad offers better mana return on picking up the coin.

With the extra gold, I recommending buying one or two Faerie Charms. This should give you enough mana regeneration to do well returning to lane. You should expect pressure from other lanes and the enemy jungle, so saving about half of your mana pool to react is crucial.

After you have enough mana regen for laning phase, you want to purchase Sightstone before the end of laning phase. It's important to keep vision up once you start roaming or if the enemy team starts disappearing from their lanes.

By the end of laning phase, you'll want to upgrade the 2 Faerie Charms to a Forbidden Idol and pick up Boots.

Mid Game and the Core Items

  • Boots of Lucidity600g

In most cases, you want to finish upgrading your Sightstone to Eye of the Watchers as soon as possible because of the extra vision and CDR it provides.

Alternatively, you can rush Ardent Censer before upgrading Frostfang, which will unlock Stoneborn Pact's power even earlier in the game.

Next, you want to upgrade your Boots to CDR boots and pick up the Redemption. That will bring you to 40% CDR from items and give you enough utility in the game.

Zilean doesn't heal, so why Ardent Censer?

On its own, Ardent Censer is not a good item for Zilean. Zilean's only healing spell comes from Chronoshift, which requires a teammate (or yourself) to die to get the heal from it. So why do I recommend Censer?

Stoneborn Pact is a Keystone Mastery in the Resolve Tree that brands targets marked by your crowd control. When your allies attack an enemy that is branded, they get a small heal over time, which is enough to trigger Ardent Censer.

Zilean has an incredible uptime on crowd control, from Double Bomb stuns to his point-and-click slow. In a grouped teamfight, Ardent Censer + Stoneborn Pact can provide an incredible advantage to your ADC or other attack speed based champions.

With this build specifically, the heal is amplified by the bonus % healing from Redemption, Ardent Censer, and Mikael's Crucible. The 40% healing buff adds about 25 health per second in a late game teamfight for allies that use any damaging ability against an enemy marks with Stoneborn Pact. If the fight goes long, the more healing Stoneborn Pact provides for your team.

Completing the Core Build

Choose 1
  • Athene's Unholy Grail2100g

  • OR

  • Mikael's Crucible2100g

Athene's Unholy Grail is an acceptable choice for Zilean, offering bonus AP and a little extra healing with your Ultimate/Redemption/Stoneborn Pact. For general purposes, this is the item you will buy in most situations.

Mikael's Crucible is great for team compositions that have heavy engage or crowd control. If you are going against an Ashe, you will want to buy this item and may purchase it before you buy Redemption. The CC cleanse is nice, but takes practice and game knowledge to use it perfectly.

Extra Utility

Locket of the Iron Solari now scales with BONUS HP, which doesn't offer much with this item build. There are times where you need the extra shielding, and it also triggers Ardent Censer on all allies around you.

Pushing Items

Banner of Command and ZZ'Rot work as shoving items. Banner of Command is better on offense, and ZZ'Rot is better for defense (by holding back waves).

Defensive Items

Banshee's Veil is a great defensive item against teams looking for picks and also gives you some extra AP in fights.

Zhonya's Hourglass is great against heavy AD teams or assassins. Since this build has very little armor, so any extra armor is highly valued.

The "Blow Stuff Up" Build

  • Eye of the Watchers
  • Boots of Lucidity
  • Morellonomicon
  • Rabadon's Deathcap
  • Seraph's Embrace
  • Oracle Alteration

This build is much more fun to play, especially in lane, but does have some hefty drawbacks.

You'll be doing plenty of damage, and most likely will be getting kills. The drawback is that you will also accidentally take CS and kills from the ADC, so you'll be putting them in a tough position.

With this item build, you are looking to get ahead in the game and become a major damage threat. If you fall behind, this build does not work out well, since you are setting yourself and your lane partner back even further.

But hey, sometimes its fun to stomp a lane and top the damage chart post game.

Starting Items

  • Spellthief's Edge350g
  • 3 Potions150g
  • Ward TrinketFREE

This item set is useful in lane for playing aggressive. Since you should be maxing Time Bomb first with this item build, you should get a decent income from this.

Just be wary that your auto attacks will draw minion attention so you will want to keep brush control and kite back so you can drop minion agro.

First Back and Early Game

  • Tear of the Goddess750g
  • Frostfang500g
  • Sightstone800g
  • Tier 1 Boots300g

This build is similar to the standard build, but delays the Sightstone even longer for more power around level 5.

The best case scenario is to get a quick kill or assist early (around level 3) and then quickly buy your Frostfang while they are running back to lane.

Some people elect to not take Tear of the Goddess at all, but I strongly advise taking the Tear before Sightstone because Tear allows you to keep your mana pool high enough to harass. Time Bomb reveals fog of war, so you can throw it in a brush to have some temporary vision, lessening the dependency of warding the side brush.

Core Items and Components

  • Eye of the Watchers550g
  • Boots of Lucidity600g
  • Fiendish Codex900g
  • Morellonomicon2000g
  • Control Wards75g
  • Oracle AlterationAT LEVEL 9

I have had great success with the quick upgrade to Eye of the Watchers. The fourth ward relieves a ton of stress about warding properly, especially if you've gotten used to the old Sightstone having a base of 4 charges.

From there, you want to power up your CDR to 45% along with getting extra AP and mana regen. Morellonomicon is the best item to complete after sightstone, providing mana, AP, and regen when you level up. From there, you want to build a juicy AP item.

Completing the Core Build

  • Tear of the GoddessUPGRADE
  • Needlessly Large Rod1250g
  • Seraph's Embrace1100g
  • Needlessly Large Rod1250g
  • Rabadon's Deathcap2550gTotal

From here, you want to finish off your Archangel's (which will immediately turn into Seraph's since you picked up Tear so early). This will give you a good boost up to about 250 AP and give you an active item for a shield.

Next, you want to save up to buy a Needlessly Large Rod as your 5th item slot, and then you want to hold that 6th item slot for a pink ward, or if you are getting very close to finishing the Deathcap, pick up the Blasting Wand.

These two items will put you over 500AP as a support, which is daunting for most squishies without MR. When you finish your final item, you can be looking at over 700AP with a flask on.

The 6th Item

  • Control Wards75g
  • Void Staff2650g
  • Zhonya's Hourglass2900g
  • Banshee's Veil2900g

As was mentioned before, the 6th item is crucial for a support. For the majority of the game, your 6th item slot SHOULD be reserved for pink wards.

Once you have all of your items completed, you can think about taking a final offensive/defensive item for your last slot.

When you are blowing stuff up, you need to do even more damage. This is where Void Staff comes in to make your Time Bombs even more potent. With this item, a Double Bomb combo may be enough to 1-shot an enemy ADC. Void Staff also helps you deal meaningful damage to tanks; a big problem that Mid Lane Zilean faces as well.

Keep in mind that Zhonya's Hourglass and Banshee's Veil both give 10% CDR, so you want to plan your build accordingly to not go over the CDR cap.

Banshee's Veil is the pick if they have AP Divers, such as Lissandra, Katarina, Diana, who will annihilate anyone without Magic Resist.

Items I Do Not Recommend Buying

Frost Queen's Claim
2200g

As tempting as it is to upgrade your gold generation item into the active item, it simply is not worth it.

The major problem with this item is that it takes up item slot on a build that does not have much extra space for items, so you have to be more conservative with your back purchases so you still have room for wards.

Building into Eye of the Watchers is recommended because it is item slot efficienct.

Talisman of Ascension
2400g

Aside from Ancinet Coin being a bad pick for Zilean, I do not recommend taking the active item upgrade from this item.

Once again, this is another case that comes down to item slot efficiency vs. an extra active item ability.

The active is somewhat wasted on Zilean because of the depreciation of movement speed past 490ms, and you can speed two people away at a higher speed. This item is more useful for speeding up the entire team.

Hextech GLP
3000g

For reasons similar to Rod of Ages, I dislike this item. The item has a higher price tag than Rod, but has no scaling and a decent active.

The biggest appeal for this item is the active. Zilean has great mobility and can "gun down" the enemy front line and slow them. Alternatively, you can just walk up to an enemy and slow them with Time Warp.

The problem with is that this is a "win-more" item; an item that works well if you are already winning, but hinders you (in terms of poor gold investment) when you are losing.

Luden's Echo
3200g

As far as AP items go, Luden's is a good choice for poke champions.

The biggest drawback to this item is that it takes an item slot that should really be reserved for either Void Staff or another magic penetration item. Zilean has a difficult time wearing down tanks, and magic penetration is vital if you are going a damage build.

If for some reason the enemy team doesn't have any Magic Resist, then this isn't a terrible buy.
