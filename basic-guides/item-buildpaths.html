Item Buildpaths: Optimizing your Items

There are many different schools of thought on how to play the game and itemization, and these three builds are what I prefer to use.

As a utility support, your job is to keep your carries alive and out of danger, provide utility to the team through vision, and be dangerous enough to wield attention from the enemy team.

As an aggressive support, your job is to trade damage effectively, while not hindering your laner by losing too much health or accidentally taking CS.

With that in mind, the best items that have worked for me are items that allow Zilean to spam out his spells without worrying about mana issues, have activatable items to empower your carries, and have mobility/escape to set up wards safely.

But sometimes it's fun to just blow people up. So we've got an item build for that too!

Just a forewarning, if you do focus damage output, there are certain downfalls to building mostly AP. Zilean has a very tough time damaging tanks, but can put some hefty damage down on squishier targets.

This item build is the most versatile of the 3 featured builds. These items offer a good mix of damage and utility. You also have the option to change your build around depending on what you are playing against.

I really like this item set because the components are low cost, meaning you won't have to hold onto large amounts of unused gold to make a purchase.

The downside is that you are reliant on teammates following up and coordinating well. In lower elos, this may not be feasible. I do recommend this if you are queued with a Marksman that you have experience with and can communicate to.

The high amount of mana, cooldown reduction, and active skills are fantastic with Zilean's kit, allowing incredible combat control and longevity in teamfights.

From here, you should choose either Zeke's Harbinger, Mikael's Crucible, Ardent Censer or Locket of the Iron Solari. You should make this decision depending on the enemy's team composition.

Zeke's Harbinger is great for empowering your AD or AP Carry, and works wonderfully with Zilean. There's a much more detailed explanation below.

Mikael's Crucible is an empowerment item, like Zeke's, but is used to save someone who gets caught by CC. The secondary stats on this item are alright, but the power comes from the CC removal and semi-immunity to additional CC. You want to buy this when the enemy team can easily lock down someone, or if your ADC keeps mispositioning.

Ardent Censer is incredible if you have the Stoneborn Pact mastery. It empowers your AD teammates, granting even more attack speed and life drain on hit.

Locket of the Iron Solari is a fantastic item for group fighting, but does not have the 10% CDR that the other items have. To get the most out of this item, I recommend running 10% flat CDR runes.

What's the Big Deal about Zeke's Harbinger?

Zeke's Harbinger is an incredibly strong support item that grants 50% critical strike chance AND 20% Ability Power for 5 seconds when fully charged.

Zilean is because he is almost always casting spells with short cooldowns, which means a high uptime for Zeke's Harbinger.

1 charge is gained per second passively, and 4 are gained from an auto attack, and 8 from each spell cast. A common 4-spell combo nets 32 charges in 1 second.

Many of the current AD Carry builds crest at 50% critical chance with Inifinity Edge and any of the Zeal Upgraded Items. If they have ONLY these two crit items, then keep the Zeke's on them.

When your ADC has more than 50% crit, switch it over to an AP Carry. This grants an extra 20% AP, so at 400AP, they get a free Needlessly Large Rod (80AP).

At 600, they get an extra 120 AP.

A full build AP that has 790-820 AP (with flasks) would be pushed close to the 1000 AP mark, making them effectively a 6.5 item carry.

The CDR Conundrum

Most of the remaining items have some defensive power with CDR attached to it. If you are worried about CDR efficiency, the cheapest alternative is to swap out to another pair of boots (570g) to not go over the CDR cap. If you'd rather keep the Summoner Spell Cooldown, then you can replace your Eye of the Oasis/Watchers for a Ruby Sightstone (~900g).

Once you have that figured out, you can go into Zhonya's, Mikael's, or Banner without worry. Just remember to make sure to buy the defensive item that makes the most sense against the composition you're playing against.

Or just try another Defensive Item

If you don't feel like selling items to avoid going over the CDR cap, you can always buy Guardian Angel, Banshee's Veil or ZZ'Rot. These items are fine, but once again itemize against what you are playing against.

The LCS / Utility Build

  • Eye of the Oasis
  • Boots of Lucidity
  • Redemption
  • Ardent Censer
  • Mikael's Crucible
  • Oracle Alteration

This item build is the pure utility build, and is even emulated in the LCS on the better Zilean supports.

There isn't much flexibility with items with this build, but these items greatly empower your teammates and force enemies to make you a priority focus target (meaning more enemy resources are spend on you instead of on your carries).

At higher elos and with coordinated teams, this is the best item build that you can create.

Starting Items

  • Ancient Coin350g
  • Refillable Potion150g
  • Ward TrinketFREE

Certain matchups for Zilean allow you to take Spellthief's Edge, but for the most part Zilean wants to just survive lane phase and have enough money to buy the next item.

I highly recommend the Ancient Coin and the Refillable Potion start. This should give you enough sustain for you to survive lane easily, and enough passive gold generation to easily reach your item on your first back.

Zilean doesn't heal, so why Ardent Censer?

On its own, Ardent Censer is not a good item for Zilean. Zilean's only healing spell comes from Chronoshift, which requires a teammate (or yourself) to die to get the heal from it. So why do I recommend Censer?

Stoneborn Pact is a Keystone Mastery in the Resolve Tree that brands targets marked by your crowd control. When your allies attack an enemy that is branded, they get a small heal over time, which is enough to trigger Ardent Censer.

Zilean has an incredible uptime on crowd control, from Double Bomb stuns to his point-and-click slow. In a grouped teamfight, Ardent Censer + Stoneborn Pact can provide an incredible advantage to your ADC or other attack speed based champions.

With this build specifically, the heal is amplified by the bonus % healing from Redemption, Ardent Censer, and Mikael's Crucible. The 40% healing buff adds about 25 health per second in a late game teamfight for allies that use any damaging ability against an enemy marks with Stoneborn Pact. If the fight goes long, the more healing Stoneborn Pact provides for your team.

The "Blow Stuff Up" Build

  • Eye of the Watchers
  • Boots of Lucidity
  • Morellonomicon
  • Rabadon's Deathcap
  • Seraph's Embrace
  • Oracle Alteration

This build is much more fun to play, especially in lane, but does have some hefty drawbacks.

You'll be doing plenty of damage, and most likely will be getting kills. The drawback is that you will also accidentally take CS and kills from the ADC, so you'll be putting them in a tough position.

With this item build, you are looking to get ahead in the game and become a major damage threat. If you fall behind, this build does not work out well, since you are setting yourself and your lane partner back even further.

But hey, sometimes its fun to stomp a lane and top the damage chart post game.

Starting Items

  • Spellthief's Edge350g
  • 3 Potions150g
  • Ward TrinketFREE

This item set is useful in lane for playing aggressive. Since you should be maxing Time Bomb first with this item build, you should get a decent income from this.

Just be wary that your auto attacks will draw minion attention so you will want to keep brush control and kite back so you can drop minion agro.

This build is similar to the standard build, but delays the Sightstone even longer for more power around level 5.

The best case scenario is to get a quick kill or assist early (around level 3) and then quickly buy your Frostfang while they are running back to lane.

Some people elect to not take Tear of the Goddess at all, but I strongly advise taking the Tear before Sightstone because Tear allows you to keep your mana pool high enough to harass. Time Bomb reveals fog of war, so you can throw it in a brush to have some temporary vision, lessening the dependency of warding the side brush.

As was mentioned before, the 6th item is crucial for a support. For the majority of the game, your 6th item slot SHOULD be reserved for pink wards.

Once you have all of your items completed, you can think about taking a final offensive/defensive item for your last slot.

When you are blowing stuff up, you need to do even more damage. This is where Void Staff comes in to make your Time Bombs even more potent. With this item, a Double Bomb combo may be enough to 1-shot an enemy ADC. Void Staff also helps you deal meaningful damage to tanks; a big problem that Mid Lane Zilean faces as well.

Keep in mind that Zhonya's Hourglass and Abyssal Scepter both give 10% CDR, so you want to plan your build accordingly to not go over the 45% CDR cap.

Abyssal is the pick if they have AP Divers, such as Lissandra, Katarina, Diana, who will annihilate anyone without Magic Resist. Zilean doesn't gain any MR through levels, so he can get bursted down quickly. Banshee's is similar, but focused purely on defense rather than bringing more AP to the build.

Items I Do Not Recommend Buying

Frost Queen's Claim
2200g

As tempting as it is to upgrade your gold generation item into the active item, it simply is not worth it.

The major problem with this item is that it takes up item slot on a build that does not have much extra space for items, so you have to be more conservative with your back purchases so you still have room for wards.

Building into Eye of the Watchers is recommended because it is item slot efficienct.

Talisman of Ascension
2500g

Once again, this is another case that comes down to item slot efficiency vs. an extra active item ability.

The active is somewhat wasted on Zilean because of the depreciation of movement speed past 490ms, and you can speed two people away at a higher speed. This item is more useful for speeding up the entire team.

Athene's Unholy Grail
2250g

Athene's Unholy Grail offers 20% CDR and 40AP, but the other effects are largely wasted for Zilean. While 20% is pretty nice, you severely limit yourself on what items you can buy after to reach CDR cap. The main passive from Athene's is mostly wasted on Zilean because his heal only triggers on resurrection.

If you are thinking about going Athene's, buy a Morellonomicon instead.

Rod of Ages
2600g

This is a very strong item for Zilean, but is more common in the midlane as an alternative to going Tear of the Goddess.

There are several Support Zilean mains that opt to buy Sightstone and go straight into Catalyst/RoA. They also play Zilean with an AP focus, but that is not the playstyle that I have had as much success with.

The reason I dislike this item is because it demands your first 2600 gold to be dedicated to getting this item up and running before you start building anything else.

Rod of Ages takes 10 minutes to power up, and depending on how late you complete it, the game may be over by then. For a support, investing 2600g without getting any CDR is painful.

With that being said, if you have a massive lead in lane (i.e. 2-3 kills before first back), it may be worthwhile to build the components and continue dominating lane. If you are behind, this item will not bring you back into the game.

If you have the income to complete this item around 15 minutes, you're in good shape. If you don't have the income by then, you're not going to be the one carrying the game through damage.

Hextech GLP
3000g

For reasons similar to Rod of Ages, I dislike this item. The item has a higher price tag than Rod, but has no scaling and a decent active.

The biggest appeal for this item is the active. Zilean has great mobility and can "gun down" the enemy front line and slow them. Alternatively, you can just walk up to an enemy and slow them with Time Warp.

The problem with is that this is a "win-more" item; an item that works well if you are already winning, but hinders you (in terms of poor gold investment) when you are losing.

Luden's Echo
3200g

As far as AP items go, Luden's is a good choice for poke champions.

The biggest drawback to this item is that it takes an item slot that should really be reserved for either Void Staff or another magic penetration item. Zilean has a difficult time wearing down tanks, and magic penetration is vital if you are going a damage build.

If for some reason the enemy team doesn't have any Magic Resist, then this isn't a terrible buy.
