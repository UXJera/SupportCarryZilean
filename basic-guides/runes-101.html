Runes 101

Runes are the early game additions that give you an advantage during the early game and laning phasem until the point where items are more defining to your champion.

There are three sets of runes for Support Zilean that are recommended depending on your playstyle. The first is a standard support mage runepage that works great in every situation. The second is a full AP page that looks to bully the enemy duo lane, and the final is a scaling CDR page that works defensively in lane.

  • Magic Penetration7.8 MPen
  • Health72 HP

When to use these runes:

When you want to use a page usable by several different supports.

When you want a balanced damage and defensive page.

When you are learning Support Zilean.

Pros
  • Balanced runepage both offensively and defensively
  • Common runepage used by several support mages
  • Leaves open options to build into offensive or utility items
Cons
  • No magic resist, trouble vs other support mages

This runepage is the common runepage set up for mages played as supports and works well in most matchups. This is a balance from the defensive page and the full damage page, which fits in with all playstyles.

If you are starting to learn Support Zilean or are playing support mages in general, this is the perfect page for beginners and veterans alike.

The main strength of this page is that it gives you flexibility to go into any item build or skill order. It allows you to play aggressively or reactively.

Defensive Runepage

  • REDS

  • YELLOWS

  • QUINTS

  • Magic Penetration7.8 MPen
  • Health72 HP
  • Magic Resist12.1 MR
  • Armor12.8 Armor

When to use these runes:

When you have an unfavorable lane matchup.

When you want to survive lane and not worry about damage.

When you are going against heavy poke or AP mages.

Pros
  • Durable laning phase
  • Has extra MR to help survive later in game
  • Can build into more damage or utility
Cons
  • Low damage output
  • Encourages a passive playstyle

This runepage is one you can also use when you are starting out to learn Support Zilean or if you are in against a heavy poke or AP mage lane.

Zilean's weakest point in the game is pre-6. He is limited by mana, spell cooldown, and lack of items to aid him. These runes help weather the storm and make it a little harder to be killed in lane.

Playing super passively makes it harder for your ADC to CS comfortably and also leads to more of the damage focus onto the ADC. This can lead to de-synced backs, a reliance to stay in lane with your ADC while the enemy support roams, and a gold deficit.

But once gain, there are going to be games where the enemy lane is overwhelmingly powerful and you are forced into this playstyle. I don't recommend this page for every single game, but for those matchups where you are going to have a very rough lane.

When to use these runes:

When you are planning to start Coin (for 20% CDR coming to lane).

When you are planning to max Time Warp (E) first.

When you want to choose different tier 2 boots.

Pros
  • Comes to lane with 20% CDR, making it easier to Double Bomb
  • Works extremely well with a full utility build
  • Works well with the max E first
Cons
  • Lower defensive and offensive stats
  • Lower poke damage during lane
  • Can tempt to use mana more liberally

The extra 10% CDR from runes makes it so you have another option on which upgraded boots you want to take. Most of the time, I go with Boots of Swiftness because it is incredibly hard to catch Zilean.

Also coming to lane with 20% CDR means you can throw single Time Bombs to poke and have it come off cooldown a few seconds earlier.

In theory this could be used in an AP focused build which gets CDR from Morellonomicon and Eye of the Watchers, but I have had great success with this build going the LCS / Utility Build.

Red Marks

Marks are the type of runes that have the best efficiency for physical offense and resistance penetration.
Best Options
  • Magic Penetration7.8 Magic Penetration
  • Attack Damage 8.5 AD

Magic penetration reds gives you the biggest bang for your buck as Support Zilean. Your Time Bombs will hit harder, and with the removal of the magic resist aura from Aegis of the Legion, cheap magic resist is inconvenient to build into for most non-tanks.

Attack Damage reds are also a viable choice for Support Zilean because your auto attack is a important part of your early laning harassment. The extra damage from AD is slightly higher than penetration runes, but becomes less effective around level 5, once enemies get the scaling armor from levels. This also helps you auto attack down minions to help push the lane or last hit if your ADC has backed.

OK to Use
  • Armor 8.2 Armor
  • Precision10.2 Lethality
    5.5 Magic Penetration

Hybrid penetration runes are great when you can mix in auto attacks. Zilean has a decent auto attack range and can weave in auto attacks easily. With the changes to Lethality (armor pen) scaling better with levels, hybrid penetration runes are much weaker than in Season 6. If you are planning on going full damage build, Magic Pen reds are a better option.

Armor reds are common for tankier supports or in lanes where you're expecting to trade often. With Zilean, it's better stay away from armor reds because you end up missing out on a potentially offensive stat that is normally provided from red runes. If you're newer or are against a heavy poke AD, armor runes should be just fine.

Yellow Seals

Seals are the most efficient slot for armor and health runes
Best Options
  • Flat HP 72 HP572 Starting HP
  • Armor 9 Armor28 Starting Armor

In most games as a support, you're either running armor yellows and HP quints, or HP yellows and armor quints. The overall cost efficiency for HP yellows is better than using armor yellows.

If you are going full damage, you will want to take the armor yellows over HP, because the trades in the bottom lane are mostly physical.

Quintessences

Even though Quints offer the greatest diversity of all the runes, there are only a handful of reasonable choices
Best Options
  • Armor13 Armor32 Starting Armor
  • Flat HP 78 HP578 Starting HP

Armor quints have a higher value than HP quints. By taking Armor Quints and HP yellows, you gain an extra 4 armor at the cost of 6hp. Because of how scalar armor works, the extra 4 armor is worth more damage reduction than 6hp. Plus as you gain more levels, your armor increases as well at a much lower rate than HP, so you'd get even less value using HP quints over Armor quints.

OK to Use
  • Flat AP 14.9 Ability Power+13 dmg to Time Bomb (+11 to players)

AP quints are solid for Midlane Zilean, but are not as good for Support Zilean. Unless you are planning on running AP blues and quints, I advise against taking these.

By not taking the defensive benefits of these runes, you have to be extra careful in lane. Missing the 13 armor or 78 HP is huge, and you could be forced out of lane easily. This means you're losing XP and harass gold, so the reward for the risk is not worth it in my opinion.

Blue Glyphs Options

Blue Glyphs are the most situational of all the runes and have the most flexibility.
This is where YOU choose what works best for you and your playstyle.
Below are the list of recommend blue runes, starting with the best at the top and descend.
Flat AP
  • Flat AP10.7 Ability Power+10 dmg to Time Bomb (+7 to players)

In most cases, taking the extra AP for lane is the right way to go.

If you are not comfortable playing aggressively or are learning Support Zilean, you may want to take Magic Resist instead just to have a higher stability.

The extra AP makes your Time Bomb hit just a little harder, and can also be used to help push a lane by throwing a single Time Bomb on the caster minions and let the ADC clear.

Magic Resist
  • Magic Resist12 Magic Resist30% Magic Dmg Reduction

The base magic resist for most champions is 30 (as opposed to armor which can be as low as 14), so you have a better buffer thanks to base stats, which makes MR runes more of a luxury.

At 30 MR, you have a 23% damage reduction to magic attacks, and with 42 that is bumped up to 30%.

Thinking long-term, these runes are quite good. Zilean has no MR gain through his levels, and the choices for MR items for Zilean are lacking.

In the lane, AP heavy "support" mages do significant damage early on (Brand, Vel'Koz, Karma, just to list a few) and every bit of MR helps you stay in lane just a little longer.

Scaling CDR
  • CDR / Level10% CDR at level 181% CDR every other level

This is an interesting set of runes that helps you as the game goes on. Early in lane, the extra CDR isn't necessary because your mana pool is too small to spam out spells, but around 15-20 minutes, then these runes pick up steam.

The big trade-off for these runes is that the number of support centric items with CDR are plentiful, making it very easy to itemize into 40% CDR. You need to be mindful of this, otherwise you end up making your items/runes less efficient.

A positive of these runes is that there are 3 extra rune slots where you can add in an extra 4 MR (which will put you at 25% magic damage resistance).

Flat Mana
  • Flat Mana101 Mana1 Free Rewind and Time Bomb

This is an interesting set that I am not completely sold on. At level 1, Zilean has a base of 361 mana, so you end up with about 25% more mana at level 1 (462 mana).

Having a larger mana pool also makes the Meditation talent more effective, meaning you'll be getting more mana back from that passive the lower your mana pool is.

The primary reason I dislike this rune set up is that if you pick up a Tear of the Goddess, these runes lose most of their value since you shouldn't have any mana problems thereafter.

Flat Mana Regen
  • Mana Regeneration3.0 Mana Regen / 5 Seconds1 Free Rewind (W) per minute

Mana regen blues were popular several seasons ago, but have lost thier value as of late. Early in the game, they do help tide you over until you have enough regen from Tear of the Goddess or Frostfang, but these quickly lose their value.

The value of these runes equate to 1 free Rewind per minute, or a free Time Bomb every other minute.

In short, these runes grant 25% mana regeneration at level 1, but as the game goes on, the runes do not scale with your mana pool so items with %-based mana regeneration are far superior.

6/3 Split Variant Blue Glyphs

Using 6 Glyphs
  • Magic Resist8 Magic Resist
  • Mana Regen2 Mana / 5
  • Flat CDR5% CDR
  • Scaling CDR10% CDR @ 18
Using 3 Glyphs
  • Magic Resist4 Magic Resist
  • Mana Regen1 Mana / 5
  • Scaling CDR5% CDR @ 18

The 8 Magic Resist is the most common compromise if you plan on mixing in only 1 Mana per 5S.

Taking 2 Mana Regen per 5 works best with the 4 Magic Resist. This diluting of MR and MReg works better for supports with fewer mana problems (Bard comes to mind), but I'd either stick w/ either all magic resist or all mana regen.

Flat CDR is more common in the jungle, but the downside is that you only get 5%. On Support Zilean, it will make things awkward where there will be a 5% CDR gap. The problem is that there are no 5% CDR items in the game anymore, so you either sacrifice the possibility of 45% CDR or you go over the CDR cap and make the CDR runes useless.

Scaling CDR's are common among midlaners, since you'll likely hit 16-18 before the game ends. Supports don't have that luxury (although Zilean's passive puts his XP much higher than other supports). It's an alright choice, but you'll have to give up a CDR item like Talisman of Ascension or Banner of Command/Mikael's in order to stay under the CDR cap.
